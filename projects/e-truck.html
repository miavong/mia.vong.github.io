01Overview

Background

The HMI Capstone sponsored by PACCAR aims to develop a safe, intuitive, and development-ready human-machine interface (HMI) for the University of Washington's E-Truck, a student-led initiative to convert a Class 7 diesel truck into an electric vehicle (EV).

Design Question

How might we design a safe, intuitive, development-ready HMI that enhances the driving experience in electric commercial trucks and supports the industry's transition to battery-electric vehicles?

02Problem

The transition to EV creates new HMI challenges

California has set the goal that 100 percent of medium- and heavy-duty vehicles in the State will be zero-emission by 2045. The current E-Truck had no digital interface capable of communicating EV-specific information to drivers.

03Solution

A dual-display HMI: A-Cluster & B-Cluster

We designed two co-existing displays — an A-Cluster driver instrument panel and a B-Cluster infotainment screen — that preserved diesel familiarity while surfacing EV-critical intelligence in real time.

07Reflection

What I learned

This project is one of my proudest builds because we worked closely with E-Truck's control engineers and PACCAR's test engineers to define and align on HMI requirements, translating complex technical constraints into clear design decisions that worked for both engineering and the end user.

The project demanded technical depth and creative problem-solving, requiring me to learn EV mechanics like regenerative braking while simultaneously self-teaching ProtoPie to prototype our UX designs. Beyond the product itself, this capstone project broke new ground for my undergrad department, marking the first-ever HCDE and PACCAR collaboration and earning a "Best Impact" award from the undergraduate showcase out of 60 teams.

Future members of E-Truck will take over our Figma designs, ProtoPie prototypes, and physical displays. The electrical and controls technical teams will handle wiring and programming the interfaces to communicate with the Electronic Control Unit (ECU) in the 2026 academic year.
